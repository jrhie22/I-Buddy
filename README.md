Found how incoming international students at GWSB had been asking very basic FAQ questions every student orientation, when I was joining as a panelist. Although commonly asked questions are well-organized
in GW websites.

A crucial question I asked myself was if there's an unknown problem causing incoming students not being able to find the most basic information and came up with a few hypothesis.
1) Given that the people asking those questions were not only incoming students (lacking general info with GW) but also international students, mostly first time visiting the U.S, seemed like they were very anxious
   when it comes to problems such as housing, making friends, difficulty of classes, etc. which cannot be answered easily from FAQs
2) For some very 'basic' questions, it's still easy to ask someone as they get accurate information much quicker.

To verify my hypothesis, I've conducted a survey with the support of G&EE team at GW School of Business.
With 35 respondents in total, I found patterns as follows:

1) Students lack full confidence — many students can find basic info but struggle navigating complex or cross-departmental processes.
2) Information access is slow and fragmented, causing delays and frustration when students seek timely answers.
3) Unclear or delayed guidance increases stress, especially for international students adapting to a new system.
